# Look Contamination Checklist

Inspect every machine asset independently and compare shared materials across views.

## Fail If Present

- inherited extreme warm/cool cast;
- crushed or unreadable shadows;
- clipped highlights that erase material;
- teal/orange or other cinematic grade;
- unnecessary sunset color;
- colored-light contamination not intrinsic to the scene;
- added bloom, flare, vignette, diffusion, shallow depth of field, black bars, grain, or camera filter;
- dramatic relighting, stylized fogging, poster/advertising composition, or beauty-shot transformation;
- cross-view base-color drift;
- shadow/highlight treatment that hides structure.

## Also Fail If Incorrectly Removed

- source-supported fixed neon or emissive architecture;
- glowing minerals or plants;
- star corona, black-hole accretion disk, jets, ring systems, nebula emission;
- wave field, current, storm structure, cloud/fog/smoke distribution, dust, snow, flood, lava, or fire when identity-defining;
- any intrinsic scene state whose removal changes the scene's identity.

## Pass Standard

The look must not obscure scene information or become the main carrier of scene identity. Intrinsic materials, colors, volume, contact shadows, emission, and identity-defining physical state remain readable and stable. Neutral does not mean gray, flat, white-box, shadowless, or final beauty-lit.
